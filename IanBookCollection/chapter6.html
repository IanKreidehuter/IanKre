CHAPTER VI

The Lantern Keeper

Three mornings later, Rowan left the valley.

There were no grand farewells.

No crowds gathering beside the train station.

No dramatic speeches.

Only a backpack, the mysterious book, and a quiet promise to himself that he would keep going until he found the answer.

🚂

The train carried him through forests older than memory.

Across rivers that glittered beneath moonlight.

Past villages whose names he had never heard before.

Days passed.

Then weeks.

And for the first time in his life, Rowan felt as though he was moving toward something rather than away from it.

🧭

The map inside the book continued changing.

Each morning new details appeared.

A road.

A bridge.

A river winding through unfamiliar land.

The closer Rowan traveled, the clearer the drawings became.

Until eventually they all pointed toward a single place.

A bridge marked with a lantern.

When the road ends, seek the lantern keeper.
🏮

One evening, after sunset, Rowan finally arrived.

The bridge stretched across a dark river.

Its stone arches reflected in the water below.

And there, exactly as the book had shown, stood a single lantern glowing softly in the darkness.

Rowan's heart began to race.

He approached slowly.

The lantern flickered.

The river whispered beneath the bridge.

And then he noticed someone standing beside the light.

✦ ✦ ✦

At first they looked like any ordinary traveler.

A long coat.

A worn satchel.

Dust from countless roads.

Yet something about them felt familiar.

Not because Rowan had met them before.

But because he felt as though he had been walking toward this moment for years.

Maybe his whole life.

"You took your time."

The stranger smiled.

Rowan blinked.

Out of all the things he expected to hear, it wasn't that.

And somehow that made him laugh.

A real laugh.

The kind that arrives before sadness has a chance to interrupt.

🌙

For a moment neither of them spoke.

The lantern glowed warmly between them.

The river flowed onward.

And the stars reflected across the dark water.

Then the traveler pointed beyond the bridge.

"Come on."
"We've still got a long way to go."

Rowan followed their gaze.

Far beyond the river.

Far beyond the darkness.

A thousand golden lights shimmered against the night sky.

A city.

Beautiful.

Ancient.

Impossible.

Everhafen.
