CHAPTER I

The Boy Who Watched Trains

A long time ago, in a valley surrounded by pine forests and distant mountains, there lived a boy named Rowan.

He was nineteen years old, though sometimes he felt ninety and sometimes he felt seven.

The people in the village said he was quiet. They said he spent too much time looking at maps, old photographs, and the stars.

They didn't know that Rowan wasn't really looking at those things.

He was looking for a place he had never seen.

✦ ✦ ✦

Every evening, when the village lights turned gold, Rowan would climb a small hill behind his house and watch the trains pass through the valley.

The trains always went somewhere.

The people inside always seemed to know where they belonged.

Rowan wondered what that felt like.

Sometimes he imagined stepping onto one of those trains and never looking back.

Sometimes he imagined arriving somewhere that felt familiar, even though he had never been there before.

And every night, as the final train disappeared into the darkness beyond the mountains, Rowan would remain sitting on the hill a little longer than everyone else.

Watching. Waiting. Wondering.
