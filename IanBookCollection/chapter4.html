CHAPTER IV

The Broken Fortress

Long before he heard the name Everhafen, Rowan had built a fortress around himself.

Not a real fortress made of stone and iron.

This one existed somewhere inside his heart.

Its walls were high. Its gates were heavy. Its towers watched every road leading inward.

Nothing entered without permission.

Nothing left without being remembered.

🏰

The fortress had not appeared all at once.

It had been built piece by piece.

A disappointment became a brick.

A betrayal became another.

Lonely winters became stone walls.

Silent evenings became iron gates.

By the time Rowan noticed what he was building, the fortress was already complete.

And for a while, it worked.

Danger remained outside.

Pain remained outside.

Nothing could hurt him.

But nothing could truly reach him either.
✦ ✦ ✦

Years passed.

Most travelers never made it beyond the gates.

Some turned around before knocking.

Some grew tired of waiting.

Some simply weren't meant to stay.

Yet every so often, someone would find their way inside.

A few stayed for a season.

A few stayed for a year.

One stayed long enough that Rowan forgot the fortress existed at all.

The gates remained open.

The watchtowers stood empty.

The walls no longer felt necessary.

🌧️

Then one day they left.

No great storm announced it.

No trumpet sounded.

No warning arrived beforehand.

They simply walked away.

And when Rowan looked around, he discovered something strange.

The fortress had been broken.

Not destroyed.

Broken.

The gates hung open.

The walls had cracks running through them.

Pieces of stone lay scattered across the ground.

And for all his effort, Rowan never figured out how to rebuild it.

Maybe he didn't want to.
🌙

That was the strange part.

Even after being hurt, even after watching people leave, even after promising himself he would never be so vulnerable again...

He still found himself hoping.

Still opening the gates.

Still believing that maybe the next traveler would stay a little longer.

Sometimes he hated himself for it.

Other times he thought it might be the bravest thing about him.

Outside his window, another train passed through the valley.

Rowan watched its lanterns disappear into the darkness.

Then his eyes drifted toward the strange book resting on his desk.

The one with the blank pages.

For a moment, he thought he saw something move across the paper.

Like ink beginning to form.

Like words trying to wake up.

Slowly, Rowan opened the book.

And this time...

The pages were no longer empty.
