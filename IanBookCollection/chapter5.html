CHAPTER V

The Book Without A Title

The words appeared slowly.

At first Rowan thought he was imagining them.

Ink spread across the blank paper like morning fog drifting over a river.

Letter by letter.

Word by word.

Until a sentence finally stood before him.

📖
Dear traveler,

You keep asking where home is.

You never ask who is carrying the lantern.

Rowan stared at the page.

The room was silent.

No footsteps. No voice. No sign that anyone else had entered.

Yet the words were undeniably there.

Fresh.

Waiting.

✦ ✦ ✦

His hands trembled slightly as he turned the page.

Another sentence appeared.

Every traveler searches for a destination.
Few notice the lanterns beside the road.

Rowan frowned.

The book answered questions with riddles.

The more he read, the less certain he became.

And yet...

For the first time in years, he felt as though something was speaking directly to him.

🏮

Night after night, the book continued writing.

Sometimes a sentence.

Sometimes only a single word.

Sometimes nothing at all.

Yet every message seemed to point toward the same truth.

Everhafen was real.

And Rowan was getting closer.

Closer than he had ever been before.

🌙

Then one evening the book revealed something new.

When the road ends, seek the lantern keeper.

Below the sentence, a small sketch appeared.

A bridge.

A river.

And a single lantern glowing in the darkness.

Rowan looked at the image for a very long time.

Because somehow...

It felt familiar.

As if he had seen it before.

Perhaps in a dream.

Perhaps in another life.

Or perhaps somewhere waiting beyond the mountains.
