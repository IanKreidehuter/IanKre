CHAPTER III

The City Called Everhafen

After that night, Rowan could think of little else.

Everhafen.

The name followed him everywhere.

He heard it in the whistle of distant trains. He saw it in old maps hanging in forgotten shops. He found himself searching for it in books that nobody else bothered to read.

The strange thing was that the city never appeared the same way twice.

🧭

Some maps placed Everhafen beyond the northern mountains.

Others marked it beside a river that didn't exist.

One ancient atlas showed an entire kingdom surrounding it.

Another didn't mention it at all.

The more Rowan searched, the less certain he became.

And yet somehow the city felt more real with every passing day.

"Maybe that's why nobody finds it."

Rowan whispered the thought aloud while sitting in the village library.

The librarian looked up briefly.

"Finds what?"
"Nothing."

The librarian nodded.

That seemed to happen often whenever Rowan mentioned Everhafen. People either laughed or changed the subject.

As though the city existed just beyond the edge of ordinary conversation.

📖

One rainy afternoon, Rowan discovered a section of the library he had never noticed before.

It sat behind a narrow staircase hidden between two shelves.

Dust covered everything.

The books looked untouched.

Most had titles written in languages Rowan couldn't understand.

Others had no titles at all.

Near the back of the room sat a single book unlike the others.

Its cover was plain.

No symbol. No title. No author's name.

Just worn leather darkened by time.

For reasons he couldn't explain, Rowan reached for it immediately.

✦ ✦ ✦

The book felt strangely warm.

As though someone had been holding it only moments before.

Rowan opened it carefully.

The first page contained only a single sentence.

"Every traveler believes he is searching for a destination until he discovers he is searching for permission."

Rowan frowned.

Permission?

Permission for what?

The book offered no explanation.

The next page was blank.

So was the page after that.

And the next.

Every page in the book was empty.

🌙

Most people would have put it back on the shelf.

Rowan carried it home.

That night he sat beside his window while rain tapped softly against the glass.

The mysterious book rested on his desk.

The blank pages seemed to wait patiently.

As though they already knew something he didn't.

Outside, a train passed through the valley.

Far beyond the mountains.

Far beyond the places marked on ordinary maps.

Somewhere out there, Rowan felt certain, the road to Everhafen had already begun.
