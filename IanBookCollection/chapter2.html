CHAPTER II

The Traveler On The Hill

One evening, after the last train had vanished into the distance, Rowan remained on the hill as he always did.

The village lights below glowed like scattered stars. The air smelled of pine trees and cold earth. Above him, the first real stars began appearing one by one.

That was when he noticed someone sitting beside him.

An old traveler.

Rowan wasn't sure where the stranger had come from. One moment the hill had been empty. The next, there was a weathered man carrying a walking stick and a lantern that seemed far older than the village itself.

✦ ✦ ✦
"Waiting for someone?" the traveler asked.

Rowan shook his head.

"No."
"Then what are you looking for?"

The question lingered between them.

Rowan thought carefully.

"A place."

The old traveler smiled.

"A place doesn't solve loneliness."
"I know."
"A person doesn't solve it either."
"I know."

The traveler studied him quietly.

"You know many things for someone still searching."

For the first time that evening, Rowan laughed. A small laugh. The kind that appeared unexpectedly.

"Knowing and believing aren't the same."

The traveler nodded slowly. As if he'd heard those words many times before.

🏮

For a while, neither of them spoke.

The wind moved gently through the pine trees. A train whistle echoed somewhere far beyond the mountains.

Then the traveler pointed toward the horizon.

"Have you ever heard of Everhafen?"

Rowan turned immediately.

Of course he had.

Everyone had.

But nobody agreed on whether it truly existed.

Some maps showed it. Others didn't.

Some called it a city. Others called it a myth.

A few believed it was simply a story told to wandering children who didn't know where they belonged.

Yet something about the way the traveler spoke made it sound real.

Dangerously real.

"They say the roads eventually lead there."
"Do you believe that?"

The traveler smiled.

"I believe roads lead somewhere."
"That's not an answer."
"Most important answers aren't."

The lantern beside him flickered softly.

For a moment, Rowan thought he saw something strange reflected inside its glass.

A city.

A river.

Hundreds of golden lights.

Then the image vanished.

🌙

When Rowan looked away for only a second, the traveler was gone.

The hill was empty again.

Only the wind remained.

And one question.

What if Everhafen was real?
