CHAPTER VII

Everhafen

The city was real.

For a long moment Rowan simply stood there, staring.

All those years of wondering. All those nights spent looking at distant trains. All those maps. All those stories.

And somehow, impossibly, Everhafen truly existed.

🏮 ✦ 🏮

The streets were lined with lanterns that glowed like captured stars.

Ancient stone bridges crossed the river.

Cathedral bells echoed softly through the evening air.

Music drifted from open windows.

People laughed.

Shops remained open long after sunset.

And nobody seemed in a hurry.

For the first time in years, Rowan felt no pressure to be anywhere except where he already was.

🌉

The traveler walked beside him through the city.

Not leading.

Not following.

Simply beside him.

The way companions are meant to walk.

Together they crossed bridges.

Shared meals.

Watched riverboats pass beneath lantern light.

And spoke about everything and nothing.

The road behind them.

The road ahead.

Dreams.

Regrets.

Memories.

"You know..."

The traveler looked toward the river.

"Most people think Everhafen is special because they finally arrive here."
"But that's not what makes it special."

Rowan waited.

"What makes it special?"

The traveler smiled.

"Nobody arrives here alone."
✨

Those words stayed with Rowan.

Because he suddenly realized something.

The loneliness wasn't gone.

Not entirely.

The fears weren't gone either.

Nor the doubts.

Nor the memories.

He was still the same boy who once sat on a hill watching trains.

Still the same boy who wondered where he belonged.

Still the same boy searching for home.

And yet...

Something felt different.

🌙

That night Rowan stood on a bridge overlooking the river.

The city lights reflected across the water.

The lanterns glowed warmly.

And for the first time in a very long while, the future didn't seem frightening.

Not because he suddenly had all the answers.

But because he no longer felt like he had to carry every question by himself.

Beside him, the traveler rested their arms on the stone railing.

The river continued flowing toward places neither of them had seen yet.

And somehow that felt comforting.

Because journeys weren't meant to end.

Only continue.
